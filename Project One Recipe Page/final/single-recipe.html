Naija Jollof

Nigerian Jollof Rice is a vibrant and beloved West African dish known for its rich, reddish-orange hue and its bold, complex flavors. It features a base of tomato sauce infused with aromatic spices like thyme, curry powder, and paprika, which give it a deliciously savory and slightly spicy profile. The rice itself is cooked to a slightly sticky consistency, ensuring that each grain is coated with the flavorful tomato sauce. Nigerian Jollof is highly customizable, often including protein and vegetables, and is typically served with accompaniments like fried plantains, coleslaw, and other side dishes. It holds cultural significance and is a symbol of celebration and togetherness, making it a staple at various social gatherings and special occasions in Nigeria.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

45 min.

serving

6 servings

instructions

step 1

Rinse the rice in cold water until the water runs clear to remove excess starch. Drain and set it aside. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent. Stir in the minced garlic, followed by the red and green bell peppers. Sauté until the peppers soften.

step 2

Add the tomato paste, blended fresh tomatoes, and continue to cook, stirring frequently until the mixture thickens and the oil starts to separate from the tomato sauce. This process is crucial for the rich flavor of Jollof rice, known as "frying the tomato." Season the tomato sauce with cayenne pepper, thyme, curry powder, paprika, bay leaves, salt, and black pepper. Adjust the spices to your taste. Add the rice to the pot and stir to coat it with the tomato sauce and spices.

step 3

Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth. If you're using protein (chicken, shrimp, beef), add it at this stage. Ensure that the liquid covers the rice and protein by about half an inch. If you're using vegetables, add them now. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the Jollof rice simmer for about 25-30 minutes. Check occasionally and add more broth or water if needed to prevent burning or sticking. Stir gently. Once the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed, remove the bay leaves, fluff the rice with a fork, and serve hot.

ingredients

2 cups long-grain parboiled rice

1/4 cup vegetable oil

1 onion, finely chopped

1 green bell pepper, finely chopped

1 can of tomato paste (400g)

2-3 fresh tomatoes, blended

1 1/2 cups dry pancake mix

2 cups chicken or vegetable broth

Salt and black pepper to taste

tools

Hand Blender

Large Heavy Pot With Lid

Cooking spoon

Gas Stove
